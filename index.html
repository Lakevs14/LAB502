Going Over Macro-Nutrients



The following information will go over more details breaking down Macro-Nutrients and their functionality.

By: Kevin E. Morales



Protein

Protein is a macronutrient that consists of amino acids which repair broken cells within the body. For the purposes of exercise science regarding powerlifting, bodybuilding etc. consuming appx. 1.8-2.2 grams per kg of bodyweight will suffice.

Carbs

Carbohydrates are important as a primary source of fuel. Red blood cells process this source of fuel in the most efficient manner, which if deprived, lead to the production of ketones hence ketosis. While ketosis is a good tool for some individuals who need epilepsy mitigation, an average healthy individuals benefits more off of carbohydrate intake.

Fats

Fats or lipids are extremely important as a necessary component for survival. They function to absorb essential nutrients such as fat-soluble vitamins like vitamins A, D, E, and K. Cell membranes within the body also use fat to maintain their structure and integrity.
